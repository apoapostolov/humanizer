# Humanizing text

Use this reference for substantial rewrites, voice preservation, audience or tone
adaptation, fiction, dialogue, and long-form editing. It organizes humanization as
one editing process rather than a hunt for isolated AI tells.

## 1. Define what must survive

Before changing the prose, record its contract:

- literal meaning and requested outcome
- facts, quotations, sources, figures, and named entities
- technical terms and distinctions
- supported uncertainty and limits
- point of view, tense, dialect, and spelling variety
- characteristic directness, warmth, skepticism, humor, or roughness
- required length, structure, formatting, and publication style

Separate defects from identity. A blunt draft should remain blunt. A technical
draft should remain precise. A personal draft may be uneven because the writer is
thinking, not because the prose is broken.

Choose an intervention before editing:

1. **Cleanup:** Keep the structure and repair local problems.
2. **Rewrite:** Preserve the message but rebuild weak sentences and paragraphs.
3. **Voice match:** Infer stable traits from a sample and apply them without
   copying conspicuous phrases.
4. **Critique:** Explain what weakens the draft and how to repair it.

When the user brings their own writing and asks for polishing rather than a full
rewrite, default to cleanup. Make a change only when you can name the reader-facing
problem it solves. Treat unusual cadence, repetition, abstraction, and small rough
edges as possible fingerprints before treating them as defects.

## 2. Write for the actual reader

Identify what the reader needs to understand, feel, decide, or do. Put that before
ceremonial framing. Match the form to the reading situation.

| Context | Lead with | Usually remove |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Technical documentation | operation, condition, exact term | promotional framing and decorative personality |
| Internal memo | decision, owner, consequence, next step | stakeholder ceremony and vague alignment language |
| Social or professional post | observation, context, credible stance | milestone theater and generic inspiration |
| Sales copy | audience problem, specific benefit, evidence | superlatives, fake urgency, and unsupported scale |
| Academic or analytical prose | sourced claim, reasoning, calibrated limit | fake authority and empty abstraction |
| Fiction | embodied point of view, selective detail, pressure | explanations of every feeling and motive |
| Personal writing | the writer's diction, priorities, and honest tension | polished sentiment imported from nowhere |

Honor platform expectations without forcing every text into a casual voice.

## 3. Ground claims in content

Replace praise, abstraction, and borrowed authority with information the reader can
evaluate.

- Name who did what and what changed.
- Prefer exact nouns and active relationships over prestige words.
- Support significance with consequence rather than adjectives.
- Attribute claims to a real source when the source matters.
- Match the strength and scope of each verb to its evidence. A limited comparison
  may *suggest* or *show* a result; it does not *prove* a universal claim.
- Name the baseline behind thresholds, ratings, and words such as *healthy*,
  *strong*, or *significant*.
- Explain why a citation matters instead of using a long citation list as borrowed
  authority.
- Keep uncertainty that follows from the evidence; remove empty hedge stacks.
- Choose one telling detail instead of inventing a decorative catalog.
- State the actual scope instead of claiming completeness.

Do not create specificity from nothing. If the draft lacks the fact needed for a
credible claim, generalize honestly, flag the gap, or ask for context. Do not turn
missing information into a claim that someone is private, keeps a low profile, or
probably had a plausible background.

## 4. Preserve voice and point of view

Remove generic language without replacing the writer with a generic “human”
persona. Preserve:

- characteristic sentence length and cadence
- degree of formality and directness
- regional language and established terminology
- deliberate fragments, repetition, understatement, or rough edges
- humor, impatience, warmth, skepticism, and restraint
- domain-specific precision
- uncertainty the evidence genuinely requires

Restore personality through choices supported by the source:

- take a clear stance when the genre permits it
- acknowledge a real tradeoff, doubt, conflict, or unresolved point
- keep useful asymmetry instead of balancing every claim
- name what is awkward, risky, frustrating, impressive, or unfinished
- use first person only when the writer or genre supports it
- allow gaps in self-knowledge when they belong to the speaker

Do not invent memories, feelings, observations, biography, quirks, or sensory
experience. Voice comes from selection and judgment, not fabricated intimacy.

## 5. Rebuild movement

Let syntax follow the thought rather than a learned cadence.

- Put the noun and main verb early when qualifiers obscure the point.
- Vary sentence length because emphasis and reasoning vary, not by quota.
- State the strong claim directly instead of using repeated negation-correction.
- Prefer plain verbs when ornate alternatives add no meaning.
- Repeat the correct technical term instead of rotating synonyms.
- Keep a rhetorical dash, triad, fragment, or parallel structure only when it
  creates a useful turn, rhythm, or distinction.
- End on the fact, consequence, image, or decision—not a trailing hedge or polished
  balance.

Read the passage aloud. Repair places where the prose feels uniformly smooth,
breathless, padded, or conspicuously engineered. Then inspect the cleanup itself:
if every paragraph now lands on a punchline, balanced contrast, or clipped
fragment, the rewrite has replaced one template with another. Remove repeated
substitute phrases instead of standardizing on a new tic.

## 6. Shape paragraphs and lists

Make each paragraph perform one useful unit of thought. Do not force every
paragraph into topic sentence, explanation, and summary.

- Start where the thought becomes useful.
- Cut bridge sentences when order already supplies the connection.
- Delay background until it changes the current claim or decision.
- Stop when the paragraph has done its work.
- Let an ending land without explaining the lesson.

Treat enumeration as structure, not decoration. Keep a series when it is a real
set with a useful order. Otherwise select, group, sequence, or change form.

| Material | Better relationship |
| --- | --- |
| atmosphere labels | material detail plus effect |
| causes and outcomes | causal sequence and intervention point |
| procedures | numbered operations with conditions and exceptions |
| comparisons | shared criteria in a list or table |
| inventory | quantity, owner, condition, use, or absence |
| dialogue or testimony | what this speaker would notice under pressure |

An inline list of four or more should have one conceptual kind, visible order, and
a reason to remain inline. Use a vertical list or table when scanning matters.

## 7. Handle narrative and dialogue

Humanize fiction through plausible attention, incomplete knowledge, and selective
detail—not casual slang or random errors.

### Interiority

- Render emotion through attention, gesture, sensation, avoidance, or a changed
  decision when that is more revealing than a label.
- Keep direct emotion words when blunt self-naming fits the voice.
- Match reflection to what the character could plausibly know about themselves.
- Trigger memory through a specific object, phrase, action, or sensation; do not
  use flashback as an explanatory essay.

### Dialogue

- Give speakers distinct cadence, vocabulary, assumptions, and omissions.
- Remove facts that informed characters recite only for the reader.
- Use *said*, *asked*, no tag, or an action beat unless a marked sound matters.
- Let recaps serve a real action such as checking an order or challenging a claim.
- Avoid cartoon dialect, uniform polish, and decorative emotional tags.

### Setting and structure

- Anchor atmosphere to a body, task, tool, or choice.
- Select details that affect action instead of inventorying the world.
- Give communities, institutions, beliefs, settings, and creatures pressures that
  exist beyond the protagonist.
- End on consequence rather than reassurance, symmetry, editorial closure, or a
  stated lesson unless the genre earns it.

## 8. Remove delivery residue

Inspect the finished text as a published artifact:

- remove assistant pleasantries, response announcements, refusals, and offers
- resolve placeholders, sample fields, subject-line scaffolding, and unfinished
  lists
- repair abrupt cutoffs and sudden changes in voice
- verify quotations and attributions
- match surrounding punctuation, capitalization, heading style, and formatting
- remove decorative headings, emoji, bold labels, or list structures that do not
  help the reader navigate

Compare the result with the original. Confirm that every factual change is
supported and every stylistic change serves the brief.

## Calibrate tone

Treat tone as a constraint on selection and rhythm, not a cosmetic filter.

- **Neutral:** Grounded, clear, restrained; prefer factual verbs and concrete
  claims.
- **Conversational:** Direct and natural; use contractions when they fit without
  adding chatter.
- **Reflective:** Allow doubt and tension without turning them into a lesson.
- **Minimalist:** Keep only necessary claims and details without flattening
  technical or emotional meaning.
- **Playful:** Use light wit or surprise without making serious material cute.
- **Storyteller:** Build movement through sequence, consequence, and selected
  detail without manufacturing an arc.
- **Poetic:** Use rhythm and image deliberately while keeping concrete anchors.

Compare the rewrite with surrounding text for continuity of point of view, tense,
density, polish, dialect, punctuation, technical vocabulary, warmth, and
certainty.

## Evaluate the result

Ask:

- Did the meaning, facts, sources, uncertainty, and technical distinctions survive?
- Does this sound like the same writer at their clearest?
- Does the voice fit the audience, platform, purpose, and stakes?
- Are claims supported by facts instead of ceremony or invented detail?
- Do sentences and paragraphs move according to the thought?
- Do lists have a real set, order, or reader-facing purpose?
- Do emotion, dialogue, interiority, and setting fit the speaker and genre?
- Does the ending land on substance rather than reassurance or explanation?
- Is all assistant, template, attribution, formatting, and cutoff residue gone?
- Does the output contain only what the user requested?

Stop when the prose is clear, credible, and continuous with its context. Do not
force every sentence through every technique.
